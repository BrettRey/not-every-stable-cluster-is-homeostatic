# Khalidi Reread Pass

## Source Pressure

Khalidi's 2018 paper starts from projectibility. Natural kind predicates are useful because they support reliable inferences to many other predicates, and the causal-network account is offered as the ontological basis for that projectibility. The network point is not just that a profile has internal structure. It is that core causal properties give rise to derivative properties in recurrent causal processes, and the richness and strictness of those links explain the variety and generality of the projections.

Khalidi also distinguishes causal order from mere convention or fiat. Purely conventional or blueprint-like bundles can support some projection, but if the properties are not causally structured and do not lead to further effects, their causal profile is impoverished. Social kinds can still be genuine on his account, but only where the conventional and causal are entangled or where causal history matters.

## Planned Edits

1. Revise "Network order without control" so it says Khalidi's causal graph explains projectibility, not merely diagnostic priority among properties.

2. Add the two dimensions Khalidi uses implicitly for projectibility: the variety of projections and their generality or strictness. This will make "network order" less like a decorative intermediate rung.

3. Mark the paper's broader use of `network order` as an extension of Khalidi's evidential role, not as Khalidi's own full causal-natural-kind criterion. In causal cases, the order is causal; in linguistic/social cases it may be functional, sequential, conventional, normative, or evidential, but only if it explains the declared projection.

4. Add an explicit constraint: if the analyst wants a Khalidi-style natural-kind claim, merely conventional or evidential order is not enough; causal, causal-historical, or causal-normative support still has to be shown.

5. Adjust the diagnostic grid language so the row does not reduce network order to "some upstream, central, derivative" structure. It should require an ordering relation that explains which projections are licensed.

## Expected Payoff

This should make the paper more faithful to Khalidi and strengthen the grid. The network-order step will now answer a projectibility question: why does this observed part of the profile license that expectation? It also prevents the linguistic extensions from being misread as a loose Khalidi citation.
