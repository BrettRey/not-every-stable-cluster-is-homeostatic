# Weinberger Reread Pass
Source reread: Weinberger, "Homeostasis and causal control," _Biology & Philosophy_ 41, article 18 (2026), DOI `10.1007/s10539-026-10018-8`.
## What the draft already gets right
- Homeostasis/control is not constancy.

- Direct clamping of the regulated variable can destroy the control relation.

- The governor is a calibration case, not itself a Boydian kind.

- The key projectible control result is a higher-scale relation preserved through lower-scale adjustment.

## Edits I should make
1. In the Watt governor section, stop calling shaft speed simply "the controlled variable." Weinberger's point is that speed is the lower-scale feedback variable, while the neglected higher-scale function is matching steam flow to workload or energy demand.

2. Strengthen the control section so "tracking" does not sound representational. The governor does not need an internal representation of engine speed. The safer diagnostic is dynamic coupling: a perturbation is coupled to a response pathway that preserves the projectible relation.

3. In the abstract/case-list wording, make "feedback architecture" more precise as "lower-scale feedback producing higher-scale matching." This keeps the Weinberger lesson visible before §8.1.

4. In the discussion, use "matching" language for the governor/glycemia contrast. The return-to-basin error is not just set-point fixation; it is missing the higher-scale matching relation.

## Leave alone
- The target-declaration paragraphs just added. They answer a different pressure and already fit Weinberger's intervention-range point.

- The glycemia target-constitution contrast. It is Bich/Bich-Mossio territory, and Weinberger supports it indirectly through the anti-set-point lesson.
