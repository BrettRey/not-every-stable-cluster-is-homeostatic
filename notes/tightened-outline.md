# Tightened Outline

## Thesis

Not every stable projectible cluster is homeostatic. Boyd is right that
projectibility can survive anti-essentialism, and Khalidi is right that
internal order matters. Weinberger's control-theoretic account means
`homeostasis` should be reserved for feedback-sensitive corrective control,
not every mechanism, stabilizer, or reliable co-occurrence.

## Article Shape

1. **Problem.** HPC theory often slides from projectibility to mechanism to
   homeostasis. The slide makes the view too permissive.
2. **Boyd.** Keep accommodation and projectibility as the payoff. Mechanisms
   matter because they explain why projection works.
3. **Slater / Onishi / Woodward.** Stable property clusters may support
   induction, but stability alone is too thin. Woodward helps mark the
   difference between observed stability and intervention-supporting
   invariance.
4. **Khalidi.** Network order improves on bare clustering: properties are not
   just listed but ordered. Across domains, the order may be causal,
   functional, conventional, sequential, or evidential. Still, order is not
   corrective control.
5. **Weinberger.** Homeostasis is causal control: perturbation-sensitive
   lower-scale adjustments preserve higher-scale relations. This is stricter
   than stability, network order, or mechanism-maintenance.
6. **Diagnostic grid.**
   - Stable property clusters: reliable co-occurrence supporting limited
     induction.
   - Network-ordered kinds: projectible profiles with internal causal order.
   - Homeostatic-control-secured kinds: feedback-sensitive control under
     perturbation.
7. **Calibration case: Watt governor.** Use Weinberger's own example because it
   makes the control relation explicit. Do not present it as a kind.
8. **Biological extension: glycemic regulation.** Use the normoglycemic
   regulatory profile as the candidate biological domain; feedback-loop
   descriptions can be too flat, and set-point talk is contested.
9. **Worked case 2: English interjections.** Use the interjections project as
   the contrast case: stable/projectible, historically and pragmatically
   structured, but not automatically a strict control system. Ameka, Norrick,
   and Dingemanse provide the immediate source anchors in the draft.
10. **Positive linguistic possibility: grammaticality.** Sketch what would have
   to be shown: correction, repair, acquisition, transmission, and uptake
   preserving a projectible relation across speakers and contexts.
11. **Payoff.** The diagnostic protects Boydian realism from inflation. It lets
   us keep stable and network-ordered linguistic/social kinds without claiming
   more homeostatic control than the evidence supports.

## Source Roles

- Boyd 1991/1999: anti-essentialist projectibility and accommodation.
- Hacking 1991/2007: context only, if the opening needs it.
- Slater 2015: stability-first pressure.
- Onishi and Serpico 2022: why both strict mechanisms and slender stability
  face costs.
- Woodward 2001: stability versus invariance for explanation/control.
- Khalidi 2013/2018: causal-network order.
- Weinberger 2026: Watt governor and homeostasis as causal control.
- Bich, Mossio, Soto 2020; Fitzgerald and Bean 2018; Bich and Bechtel 2022;
  Bich et al. 2016: biological control and glycemic regulation case.
- Ameka 1992, Norrick 2009, and Dingemanse 2024: contrast case for stable
  pragmatic projectibility without strict homeostatic control.

## Drafting Constraint

The article should not add more natural-kinds background until the two worked
cases are in place. Any new paragraph should answer one of two questions:

1. What does the category let us project?
2. What kind of structure secures that projection: stability, network order, or
   corrective control?
