## Substantive Compression

1. **Intro roadmap:** cut the four-case one-line roadmap from the introduction. Keep only the cross-domain scope and the sober linguistic payoff. This removes the near-duplicate of the abstract and §8 roadmap.

   Proposed replacement for the current contribution/roadmap run:

   > The answer to \textcite{onishi2022} is therefore a division of evidential labour. The original work of the paper is the \term{stabilizer}/\term{controller} split and the intermediate taxonomy around it: a maintainer can stabilize a projectible profile without registering and correcting perturbations to the relation that made it projectible. Network order and maintenance are likewise distinct evidential demands, and many real kinds may satisfy some of these demands without satisfying others.
   >
   > The cases have to show that separability. The scope is Boyd-style projectible realist kind claims. Many classifications earn their keep by mapping variation, stabilizing measurement, organizing historical comparison, or coordinating inquiry before they support strong projection. Those uses are legitimate, but the diagnostic here concerns categories offered as real kinds because they support prediction, explanation, induction, or controlled intervention.
   >
   > The paper tests the diagnostic across a machine, an organism, and two linguistic phenomena. The linguistic payoff is sober: the diagnostic mostly tells us when not to call a category \term{homeostatic}, while preserving a standard for future positive cases.

2. **§7 separability paragraph:** thin the stipulation-warning paragraph so the conclusion’s final sentence remains the full payoff.
