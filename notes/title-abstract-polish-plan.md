# Title and Abstract Polish Plan

I recommend keeping the main title and changing the subtitle to include the missing maintenance tier.

## Title

Keep:

> Not every stable cluster is homeostatic

Change the subtitle from:

> Stability, network order, and control in projectible kinds

to:

> Stability, network order, maintenance, and control in projectible kinds

This is the low-risk fix. It preserves the tier-list subtitle and stops hiding the stabilizer/controller split by omitting maintenance. I would not switch to "Stabilizers, controllers, and projectibility in natural kinds" unless we want the title block to sell the original cut more aggressively at the cost of losing the tidy evidential sequence.

## Abstract

Make four narrow edits:

1. Replace "reliable co-occurrence" with "a stable projectible profile" so the bottom tier doesn't look like mere recurrence.

2. Replace "feedback-sensitive control" with "corrective control" to match the grid and avoid seeming to conflate feedback with control.

3. Tighten "That result doesn't depend on what the top tier is called" to "That result holds whatever we call the top tier."

4. Make the case sentence contentful and easier to parse with semicolons:

> The Watt governor shows how lower-scale feedback can produce higher-scale matching; glycemic regulation shows how a regulated range can be constituted by self-maintenance; interjection tokens show projectible stability that no specified controller secures; and monitored agreement production shows what a specified linguistic loop would have to provide.

Also tighten the closer:

> The payoff is a diagnostic that keeps those achievements apart without turning \term{homeostatic} into a loose synonym for stable or projectible.

## Calls I would not take

I would not put Onishi and Serpico into the abstract. The abstract already has enough framing, and the introduction gives the journal hook immediately.

I would not change the main title. The productive tension is doing useful work, and \term{homeostatic} is the searchable venue-facing word.
