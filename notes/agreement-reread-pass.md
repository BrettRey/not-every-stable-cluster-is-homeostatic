# Agreement Reread Pass

Sources checked:

- Corbett (2006), local PDF: agreement is systematic covariance between a controller and target within a domain, with features and values. This supports the dependency vocabulary already in the section.
- Bock & Miller (1991), online PDF: agreement attraction is not a generic nearby-noun effect. The strongest finding is that plural local nouns reliably increase subject-verb agreement errors, while animacy and constituent length do not. They read this as evidence about syntactic and morphological implementation in production.
- Levelt (1983), PubMed/MPI record: self-repair proceeds through monitoring/interruption, hesitation or editing terms, and repair proper. The MPI record also notes that self-monitoring is probably based on parsing inner or overt speech, not direct access to the production process.

Planned edits:

2. Tighten the attraction paragraph: specify plural local attractor and implementation perturbation, not just “nearby mismatching noun.”
3. Tighten the monitoring paragraph: Levelt supports a perceptual/parsing-based monitoring loop, so the controller claim should not imply direct inspection of production machinery.
4. Keep the boundary conclusion unchanged: the loop can secure an utterance-level agreement relation, not agreement as a type-level grammatical kind.
