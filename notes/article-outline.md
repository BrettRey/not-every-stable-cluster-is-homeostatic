# Article Outline: Not Every Stable Cluster Is Homeostatic

Working spec for expanding the seed draft (~2,350 words) into a compact JGPS
research article (target 5,500--7,000 words of body; abstract and references
extra). Approved 2026-05-28.

## Thesis

Boydian HPC theory needs a sharper homeostasis constraint. Projectibility is
the target. The paper now orders the analysis through a diagnostic ladder:

1. **Projection target**: the inference, prediction, explanation, or intervention
   the category is supposed to support.
2. **Stable profile**: reliable co-occurrence with a specified projection.
3. **Network order**: internal ordering among central, derivative, and
   evidentially useful properties (causal, or functional/conventional/
   sequential/evidential in linguistic and social cases).
4. **Maintenance**: a stabilizer whose removal would make the profile fragment,
   drift, or dissolve.
5. **Corrective control**: feedback-sensitive correction that preserves a
   higher-scale relation through lower-scale variation.

Only the fifth earns the word *homeostatic*. The diagnostic protects Boyd's
anti-essentialism while preventing *homeostatic* from becoming a loose synonym
for stable, maintained, network-ordered, or projectible.

## Bridge concept (the hinge that connects to the programme)

The HPC book defines **stabiliser** as the functional role a process plays in
maintaining a cluster (removing it would make the category "fragment, drift, or
dissolve"), as distinct from **mechanism** (causal structure). The book's
`appendix_diagnostics.tex` already separates **homeostasis proper**
(return-to-basin, finite return time under perturbation) from **broader
maintenance** (metastability, causal isolation, frozen accidents). So "not every
stabiliser is a controller" is already latent in the formalism. This paper makes
it prose-explicit, cross-domain, and Weinberger-sharpened.

Presentation choice: introduce *stabiliser* as a general conceptual point any
JGPS reader can use, with a light third-person citation that survives
double-anonymous review. Do not foreground "my book."

## Section sequence (job + word ceiling)

1. **Introduction** (600). Ambiguity in *homeostatic* (reliable co-occurrence /
   internal order / feedback control are different achievements). Working claim:
   not every stable cluster is homeostatic; treating it so makes HPC too
   permissive. Target = projectibility; contribution = a diagnostic. Fold in the
   cross-domain-legitimacy note: the cases test one domain-neutral diagnostic,
   they are not a survey of domains. Roadmap.
2. **Projectibility first** (Boyd) (450). Projection without necessary/
   sufficient conditions; accommodation thesis. Recurrence alone is too cheap;
   must say what is projected from what.
3. **Stability is not enough** (Slater, Onishi-Serpico, Woodward) (550). SPCs
   real without correction (Diagnostic 1, modest). Onishi-Serpico: stable-cluster
   views risk losing the processes that make some inductions rational; strict
   mechanism views inherit boundary/abstraction/one-to-many problems; plus their
   "what counts as a homeostatic mechanism" circularity. Woodward invariance:
   stability under intervention, not just across space and time.
4. **Network order without control** (Khalidi) (500). Properties ordered
   (generate/sustain/constrain/explain); explains diagnostic vs derivative. But
   ordered dependence is not corrective control (Diagnostic 2). Failure mode:
   causal vocabulary for an unordered list. Network order construed broadly
   across domains.
5. **From stabilisers to controllers** (NEW bridge) (450). Maintenance is the
   umbrella; the question is which stabilisers are controllers. A controller is
   the strict subtype: feedback-sensitive corrective organization preserving a
   higher-scale relation through lower-scale variation. Reframes the three
   securings as stabiliser *types*. Sets up Weinberger.
6. **Corrective control** (Weinberger) (600). Kinds do not sense/compare/correct;
   a kind earns the label only when maintained by a controller. Homeostasis is
   not constancy; preserve a higher-scale relation through lower-scale variation;
   clamping can destroy the control relation. Four evidential marks. Bich/Bechtel:
   control mechanisms sense, transmit, alter constraints; integrated not centrally
   commanded (Diagnostic 3).
7. **The diagnostic ladder** (+ table) (450). Reverse inference fails: control
   usually brings stability, network order, and maintenance with it, but those
   lower rungs do not imply control. Table is the hinge. Five questions before
   using *homeostatic*.
8. **How to read the cases** (350). Status of each: governor = calibration (not a
   kind), glycemia = hard biological candidate, interjections = negative
   linguistic, agreement monitoring = candidate positive linguistic. Why cross-domain is legitimate for
   general philosophy of science.
9. **Calibration case: Watt governor** (450). Explicit control structure
   (flyball -> valve -> steam -> shaft speed). Clamp severs incoming relations,
   destroys control. Vary lower-scale to preserve the higher-scale relation.
10. **Biological extension: glycemic regulation** (550). Candidate is the
    normoglycemic regulatory *profile*, not glucose-as-substance or one value.
    Sensors/signals/effectors/organized adjustment (Bich, Mossio, Soto).
    Set-point vs equilibrium (Fitzgerald-Bean). Organizational closure. Counts as
    control-secured only with evidence of organized correction.
11. **Negative linguistic case: interjections** (600). Stable + projectible
    (prosodic isolation, low syntactic integration, non-inflection,
    non-referentiality, interactional force). Pragmatic payoff (Dingemanse,
    Norrick, Ameka). Likely stabilisers and some network order. The homeostasis
    question is stricter: coordinated corrective control is not shown. Makes the
    temptation visible, then blocks the overclaim.
12. **Candidate positive linguistic case: agreement monitoring** (NEW) (700).
    Agreement gives the ordered dependency: controller-target relation, target
    forms projected from controller features, and mismatch as perturbation. The
    candidate control loop is online production monitoring and self-repair, not
    Corbett's grammatical controller by itself. Acquisition and norm enforcement
    are separate possible loops unless coordination is shown. One-paragraph
    grammaticality gesture: a harder, contested candidate, left open not granted.
13. **What the diagnostic changes for HPC theory** (NEW, replaces thin
    Discussion) (550). Disciplines HPC in linguistics and social ontology:
    stable/ordered/mechanism-maintained without all being homeostatic. Objection:
    policing/repair is feedback. Concession strengthens the diagnostic: some
    linguistic/social kinds may be control-secured where correction, repair,
    acquisition, transmission, and uptake form a coordinated architecture (as
    agreement monitoring may do). Give-back to formal HPC: a homeostasis basin should
    be defined over the higher-scale *relation*, not fixed lower-scale values
    (return-to-relation, not return-to-constancy).
14. **Conclusion** (250). Reserve *homeostatic* for corrective control; protect
    Boyd's anti-essentialist insight.

Approx total body: ~7,050 (ceilings; trim setup sections first if long).
