# Jantzen Reread Pass

Jantzen (2015) sharpens the projectibility setup in two ways. First, he distinguishes system induction from kind induction: one projects across states of a system of variables, the other across systems in a class. Second, he distinguishes two questions: what makes a system or kind projectible, and how investigators can discover projectible systems or kinds.

The draft already has the projectibility-first spine, but it can use Jantzen to clarify its own scope. This paper is not trying to be a discovery algorithm for natural kinds. It starts after a candidate profile has been offered as projectible and asks what evidential securing structure has actually been exhibited.

The useful contrast is:

1. Jantzen: search for dynamical symmetries, transformations to which the causal structure is indifferent, as a route to discovering projectible kinds.
2. This paper: diagnose whether a projectible profile is merely stable, network ordered, maintained, or control-secured.
3. The connection: symmetry or invariance evidence can help satisfy the stable-profile or network-order demands, but it does not by itself show maintenance or corrective control.

Planned edits:

1. In `Projectibility first`, add one sentence after the Goodman setup noting that projection targets can be system-level or kind-level in Jantzen's sense.
2. In `Observed stability and invariance`, add a short paragraph distinguishing the diagnostic from Jantzen's discovery project and treating symmetry evidence as one possible way of exhibiting projectible structure.
3. In the conclusion or discussion, add a compact sentence saying the grid complements discovery-oriented accounts such as Jantzen's by asking what has been secured once a candidate profile is on the table.

This should help against a reviewer asking why the paper does not discuss the most explicit projection-first natural-kinds account already in the bibliography.
