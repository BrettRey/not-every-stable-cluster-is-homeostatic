# §8.3 and §8.4 Polish Plan

This pass should strengthen the home-field linguistic case without changing its verdict. The goal is to make the negative result read as principled: interjection tokens get projectibility, some order, and plausible maintainers, but the control loop is not specified at the right scale.

## §8.3 Interjection Tokens

I would restructure the opening around two levels of projection.

1. **Single token-level anchor.**

   Consolidate the current two statements into one opening:

   > English interjection tokens are the contrast linguistic case. The unit is token-level: a form counts here only when it occurs with the interjection profile of prosodic detachability, weak syntactic integration, interactional force, and uptake-relevance. A detached response cry \mention{shit} is an interjection token, while \mention{shit} in an ordinary nominal environment is a noun token.

   Then use this anchor later when discussing perturbations, without re-defining the candidate.

2. **Make broad vs constructional projection the section spine.**

   Use two paragraphs:

   - Broad-category level: weak but real expectations about prosodic detachability, low syntactic integration, non-inflection, interactional force, and uptake-relevance.
   - Lower constructional level: stronger expectations from forms such as \mention{huh?}, \mention{mmhm}, \mention{oh}, \mention{wow}, \mention{oops}, \mention{ouch}, \mention{yuck}, \mention{shit}, \mention{hey}, \mention{well}, and \mention{uh}.

   Add a bridge sentence:

   > The two-level structure matters because the diagnostic should not force projectibility to be all or nothing.

3. **Consolidate candidate loops.**

   Replace “Candidate loops should be separated” plus the separate loop paragraph with one paragraph:

   > A control claim would have to identify one loop rather than move among several possibilities. In an online interactional loop, failed or ambiguous uptake could lead to clarification or repair that restores sequential intelligibility. In a developmental loop, learner misuse could lead to correction or modelling and convergence on adult use. In a community-transmission loop, repeated uptake patterns could entrench a form--function pairing. These are neighbouring stabilizers, not yet one control architecture.

4. **Make the agreement contrast the conclusion of the case.**

   Keep the evidential-tractability contrast, but make it explicitly the reason the negative result is principled:

   > The contrast with monitored agreement production turns on evidential tractability, not on a special standard for interjections. ...

## §8.4 Monitored Agreement

Keep the scale caveat, but state it fewer times.

1. Keep the opening caveat:

   > The candidate is the monitored production process preserving an utterance-level agreement relation, not the type-level grammatical regularity by itself.

2. Compress the later scale paragraph:

   > This is where scale matters. Agreement as a type-level grammatical regularity may be stable and ordered, but online self-monitoring operates on particular utterances. Repairing \olang{The key to the cabinets are} to \olang{The key to the cabinets is} preserves this utterance's agreement relation. The case is therefore a boundary case: token-level control evidence is possible without settling control of the broader grammatical kind.

3. In §9, reduce the repeated hedge:

   > The monitored agreement case keeps that condition visible: its candidate controller is the monitored production loop preserving an utterance-level agreement relation. Broader categories would need their own coordinated architecture of correction, repair, acquisition, transmission, and uptake.

   And later:

   > monitored agreement production shows what a local control-secured loop can look like.

## Do Not Change

Do not add sources. Do not manufacture a positive linguistic kind-level result. The point is still the sober one: interjections are the negative home-field case; monitored agreement is a boundary case for a specified loop.
