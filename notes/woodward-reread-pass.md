# Woodward Reread Pass

## Source Pressure

Woodward's 2001 paper is not just a general appeal to "stability under intervention." The important distinction is between de facto stability and invariance. A generalization can be stable under many ordinary changes and still fail to be explanatory if it is not invariant under the right interventions. Common-cause correlations are the model problem: they may be stable, but intervening on one effect will not manipulate the other.

For Woodward, invariance is modal and counterfactual. It asks whether the generalization would continue to hold under some range of interventions on variables in the relation, in the same system whose behaviour is being explained. The range matters: some generalizations are invariant under some interventions but not others, and deeper explanations answer a wider range of what-if-things-had-been-different questions.

## Planned Edits

1. Revise the Woodward paragraph in "Observed stability and invariance" so it distinguishes Slater-style projectible stability from Woodward-style explanatory invariance.

2. Stop making the first stable-profile step sound as though every stable kind claim must already support interventions. The first step should be projectibility across relevant contexts; when the claimed payoff is explanation or controlled intervention, the stronger Woodwardian test applies.

3. Add the common-cause lesson in compressed form: mere stability under many changes is not enough, because the wrong intervention can break a relation or reveal that the relation was not explanatory.

4. Connect Woodward to the later clamp/control discussion. A perturbation is probative only when it tests the relation at the right place in the causal structure; clamping the regulated variable may destroy the very relation being tested.

5. Keep the paper's projectibility-first structure intact: Woodward strengthens the explanatory/intervention side of the stable-profile demand; he does not replace Slater's cliquish stability as the first diagnostic step.

## Expected Payoff

This should make the bridge from stability to control more disciplined. It will also reduce a possible reviewer complaint that the paper slides from "stable enough for projection" to "intervention-invariant" without saying when the stronger standard is required.
