- It keeps projectibility before mechanism.

- It already says the stricter control sense is normative and diagnostic, not a claim about all historical uses of "homeostatic."

## What the reread adds
Boyd 1999 distinguishes programmatic definitions from explanatory definitions. A programmatic definition states the inductive or explanatory role a kind term plays; an explanatory definition states the shared properties and causal structures that let the kind play that role. This maps almost exactly onto the paper's projection-target-first strategy.

Boyd's "homeostatic" is deliberately broad. The cluster may be sustained because some properties favor others, because underlying mechanisms maintain them, or both. So the paper should not say or imply that Boyd's homeostatic mechanism is just the stabilizer role, let alone the controller role.

Boyd also treats vague, relational, historical, and discipline-relative kinds as legitimate when they contribute to accommodation. That supports the paper's linguistic/social cases, but only when their projection target is stated.
