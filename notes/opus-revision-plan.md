# Opus Revision Plan

## Aim

Revise `main.tex` as a substantial JGPS-facing revision, not a local polish pass. The new version should make the paper's original contribution the intermediate taxonomy: projectible profile, network order, maintenance, and corrective control are distinct evidential demands. Weinberger supplies the control-theoretic apex; the paper supplies the HPC-facing diagnostic architecture and shows why linguistic cases mostly do not yet clear it.

## Load-bearing assumptions

1. The paper should not promise a positive linguistic homeostasis result. If the agreement case is framed as established control of a type-level grammatical kind, the scale objection is strong. The safer and better claim is negative-diagnostic: current linguistic cases either fall short or show what a specified loop would have to look like.
2. The "ladder" metaphor is useful but over-linear. Network order and maintenance need to be presented as partially independent evidential demands, with corrective control as the strictest case rather than as rung five of a guaranteed climb.
3. "More literally" should go. The argument is not that homeostasis means constancy in the everyday sense; it is that the term should be used more demandingly, in its control-theoretic sense.
4. The top rung is interest-relative too. The scale split between higher-scale relation and lower-scale variables must be declared case by case, or the diagnostic inherits hidden discretion.
5. The governor calibrates feedback architecture but not target constitution. Glycemic regulation and organizational closure carry the lesson about endogenous range constitution.

## Planned edits

1. **Abstract and introduction**
   - Reframe the contribution around the stabilizer/controller counterfactual split and intermediate taxonomy.
   - Make the Onishi--Serpico JGPS lineage visible in the introduction.
   - Replace "more literally" with "more demandingly" or "in its control-theoretic sense."
   - State the negative linguistic payoff plainly: the diagnostic protects projectible linguistic categories from overclaiming homeostasis.

2. **Projectibility and taxonomy**
   - Retain projectibility-first framing, but reduce repeated five-question summaries.
   - Replace the linear "ladder" language where it overreaches with "diagnostic grid," "evidential demands," or "partial order."
   - Add a short paragraph explaining that network order and maintenance are not strictly sequential.

3. **Corrective control**
   - Distribute support across Weinberger and Bich/Bechtel rather than making Weinberger the sole apex.
   - Add the scale-relativity qualification: the tracked relation, lower-scale variables, perturbation, and response pathway must be specified for the projection target.
   - Add a governor qualification: it calibrates architecture, while organizational closure handles target constitution.

4. **Cases**
   - Keep glycemic regulation as the biological stress test, but sharpen the target-constitution contrast with the governor.
   - Keep interjections as the model negative case and make it self-contained enough not to depend on inaccessible Reynolds work.
   - Recast monitored agreement production as a token-level monitoring loop that illustrates what a positive linguistic loop would need, not as established control of a type-level grammatical kind.
   - Remove or soften the unsupported SPACER model-result claim; keep SPACER only as an empirical design/proof-of-concept unless numbers are included.

5. **Discussion and conclusion**
   - Make the headline constructive but sober: the gain for linguistics is a disciplined negative result plus a specification standard for future positive cases.
   - Collapse redundant restatements of the diagnostic.
   - Preserve the paper's core thesis: stable, ordered, and maintained categories can be real and projectible without being homeostatic.

Build with `make`. Then run a house-style scan for long paragraphs, raw quotes, wrong dashes, and missing semantic macros. Also run a targeted HPC check: the revised version must keep projectibility as the payoff, not drift into mechanism taxonomy for its own sake.
