# Onishi and Serpico Reread Pass
Source reread:

- Onishi & Serpico 2022, "Homeostatic Property Cluster Theory without Homeostatic Mechanisms: Two Recent Attempts and their Costs."

## What the draft already gets right
- It starts from projectibility, not mechanism.

- It treats interest-relativity as explicit and disciplined rather than eliminated.

- It uses Slater and Khalidi as neighbouring positions: stability without mechanism and causal-network order without homeostatic control.

- It already answers the target-declaration worry with empirical exhibition: a declared target must improve projection and be shown in the dynamics.

## What the reread adds
Onishi & Serpico's paper is sharper than a generic complaint that "homeostatic mechanism" is underexplicated. Their structure is a dilemma:

1. A slender stable-cluster account captures inductive usefulness but risks being too thin to explain why further properties should be expected, especially when property realization is context-dependent.

2. A thicker causal-network account keeps causal grounding but inherits interest-relativity through graph boundary choices, abstraction level, and no one-to-one mappings between mechanisms/processes and clusters.

3. Interest-relativity is not relativism: once the purpose is specified, the world still constrains whether a taxonomy is apt for that purpose.

## Edits I should make
1. In the introduction, make clearer that the paper answers Onishi & Serpico by dividing evidential labour, not by offering one sharper general definition of "homeostatic mechanism."

2. In "Observed stability and invariance," spell out the Onishi & Serpico dilemma more accurately: Slater is too slender for some inductive inferences; Khalidi remains interest-relative because causal graphs require choices about boundaries, abstraction, and where to carve networks.

3. In "The diagnostic grid," add a sentence that the grid is the constructive response: stability, network order, maintenance, and control are separate demands because no single notion should be made to do all of them.

4. In the discussion or conclusion, tie interest-relativity to constraint: declared purposes are human, but whether a profile supports projection under that purpose is empirical.
