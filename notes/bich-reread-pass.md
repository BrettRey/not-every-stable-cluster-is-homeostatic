## What the draft already gets right
- Feedback-loop diagrams are clinically/descriptively useful but too thin as explanations of biological homeostasis.

- The feedback diagram flattens a hierarchical organism into a chain of components.

- The normal range is not simply a designer-set target in organisms.

- Organizational closure supports the idea that glucose regulation is connected to self-maintenance.

- Bich & Bechtel support distributed/heterarchical control, not a central command picture.

## Edits I should make
1. In the glycemia section, stop saying the "standard endocrine picture" supplies a control architecture too flatly. Bich et al. present it as the standard feedback representation; it is useful and control-like, but it is exactly what they go on to criticize.

2. Name the four feedback-loop limitations more accurately: neat localization, flat chain, narrow variable set, and unexplained set point/normal range.

3. Recast organizational closure as a stronger explanatory demand: glucose homeostasis is a manifestation of organismic self-maintenance and energy-resource management, not just a variable held in range.

4. Strengthen the Bich & Bechtel control sentence: control mechanisms measure variables, set constraints in the controller, and act on flexible constraints in controlled mechanisms; living-system control is organized in networks/heterarchies.
