# Literature Map

Purpose: keep the paper short and projectibility-first. The question is not
which sources are interesting, but which source does work in the argument:
stable cluster, network order, or strict homeostatic control.

## Argument Slots

1. Boyd supplies the anti-essentialist projectibility frame.
2. Slater and Onishi/Serpico create the pressure: stability and process both
   matter, but neither should be casually renamed homeostasis.
3. Khalidi supplies internal causal/network order without requiring literal
   homeostatic return.
4. Weinberger supplies the stricter control-theoretic guardrail.
5. Woodward helps separate mere stability from intervention-supporting
   invariance.
6. The clean control calibration case should be Weinberger's Watt governor; do
   not present it as a kind.
7. The biological extension should use glycemic regulation as a harder case,
   not as the uncomplicated positive case.
8. The linguistic contrast case should use interjections as a stable pragmatic
   category, with Ameka, Norrick, and Dingemanse as the immediate source
   anchors.

## Do Not Chase Yet

- More Kripke/Putnam/Donellan semantics unless the paper pivots toward
  natural-kind terms.
- More organism/autonomy literature unless the biological case becomes the
  center of the paper.
- More general natural-kinds surveys. The draft's contribution is the
  stability/network/control distinction.

## Next Writing Move

Draft the paper around two contrasts:

1. Stability versus homeostatic control: Slater, Woodward, Weinberger.
2. Network order versus corrective control: Khalidi, Onishi/Serpico,
   Weinberger.

Use the Watt governor as the clean control calibration case, then glycemic
regulation as the harder biological extension. The second worked case is
English interjections: stable and projectible in pragmatic use, but not yet
shown to be maintained by corrective control. Grammaticality is the likely
positive linguistic case if the paper is expanded.
