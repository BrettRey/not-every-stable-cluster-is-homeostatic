\begin{abstract}
Homeostatic property cluster theory gave natural-kinds realism a
powerful anti-essentialist form: a kind can be real because its
properties cluster through mechanisms rather than because it has an
essence \citep{boyd1991,boyd1999}. But the word \term{homeostatic} has
often carried more weight than the theory has earned. Some stable
clusters support induction without corrective control, and some
Khalidian network nodes support projection through internal order rather
than through homeostatic return \citep{khalidi2013,khalidi2018nodes}.
This paper uses Weinberger's account of homeostasis as causal control
\citep{weinberger2026HomeostasisCausalControl} to separate three cases:
stable property clusters, network-ordered kinds, and strict
homeostatic-control kinds. The payoff is a sharper Boyd--Khalidi
division of labour: projectibility is the epistemic target, network
order explains many projectible kinds, and homeostasis should be
reserved for feedback-sensitive maintenance under perturbation.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The target is a quiet ambiguity in recent anti-essentialist kind theory.
Boyd's homeostatic property cluster framework, Slater's stable property
cluster proposal, and Khalidi's causal-network account all reject
essences. They also all aim to explain why some categories support
projection. But they differ over what does the explanatory work:
clustering, stability, internal order, or homeostatic maintenance.

The working claim of this paper is simple: not every stable cluster is
homeostatic. Treating it as such makes the framework too permissive.
The correction is not to abandon Boyd, but to make \term{homeostasis}
more literal and more demanding by using Weinberger's causal-control
account as the guardrail.

\section{The problem}

Boyd's framework works because projectibility does not require necessary
and sufficient conditions. A category can support induction when its
associated properties are held together by mechanisms. The risk is that
\term{mechanism} becomes too easy to satisfy. Any recurring stabiliser,
any institutional reinforcement, or any reliable co-occurrence can start
to look homeostatic.

Khalidi's network account addresses a different weakness. A cluster is
more than a list of associated properties. Its properties can be
ordered: some generate, sustain, or explain others. That's an advance
over a bare cluster picture, but it doesn't by itself recover
homeostasis. Ordered dependence is not the same as corrective control.

\section{Analysis}

The proposed taxonomy has three tiers.

First, stable property clusters support limited induction because their
properties co-occur reliably. They may be useful, but their stability
does not yet show a corrective mechanism.

Second, network-ordered kinds are projectible because their associated
properties occupy structured positions in a causal or mixed network.
Khalidi's view is strongest here: order explains why some properties are
diagnostic and others derivative.

Third, homeostatic-control kinds are stricter. In these cases,
feedback-sensitive mechanisms correct perturbation and preserve a
higher-scale relation through lower-scale variation. Here
Weinberger's account matters. Homeostasis is not mere constancy, but it
is also not mere stability.

\section{Discussion}

The distinction matters for Brett Reynolds's broader HPC programme in
linguistics and social ontology. Linguistic categories may be stable,
projectible, and mechanism-maintained without all being homeostatic in
the strict control-theoretic sense. Social practices may be stabilized
by records, offices, sanctions, training, and uptake without becoming
single homeostatic control systems.

The paper should therefore test its taxonomy against two worked cases:
one case from biological or engineering control, and one linguistic or
social case where stability and projectibility are real but strict
homeostasis would overstate the evidence.

\section{Conclusion}

Projectibility remains the point. Mechanisms and network order matter
because they explain why projection is reliable. Homeostasis is one
important way projection can be secured, but it isn't the name for every
stable pattern worth taking seriously.
