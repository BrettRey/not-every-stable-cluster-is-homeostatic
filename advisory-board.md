# Advisory Board

## Reviewers

### Muhammad Ali Khalidi
- **Expertise:** Natural kinds, causal-network accounts, human and social kinds, projectibility.
- **Persona:** Sympathetic to anti-essentialist kind theory but alert to whether the paper fairly distinguishes causal-network order from control.
- **Watch for:** Whether "network order without control" is a genuine distinction; whether the broadened cross-domain use of network order remains coherent; whether the paper gives projectibility enough explanatory work.

### Yukinori Onishi
- **Expertise:** Homeostatic property cluster theory, critiques of HPC without homeostatic mechanisms, stable property clusters.
- **Persona:** Close JGPS interlocutor who will press whether the diagnostic solves the problem identified in the recent HPC literature or only renames it.
- **Watch for:** Whether stability, network order, and corrective control are distinguished with non-stipulative diagnostics; whether the paper handles Slater and mechanism-sceptical HPC fairly.

### Naftali Weinberger
- **Expertise:** Homeostasis as causal control, dynamic causal models, intervention, Watt governor.
- **Persona:** Control-theory guardrail reviewer who tests whether the paper uses causal-control machinery accurately rather than as a slogan.
- **Watch for:** Whether the governor case is correctly treated as a calibration case rather than a kind; whether the paper understands control as preservation of higher-scale relations through lower-scale variation; whether the agreement-monitoring case specifies a real feedback loop rather than relying on a loose analogy.

### Marc Ereshefsky
- **Expertise:** Scientific kinds, pluralism about classification, philosophy of biology, limits of HPC theory.
- **Persona:** Broad scientific-kinds reviewer who will test whether the paper's diagnostic advances kind theory rather than only policing terminology.
- **Watch for:** Whether the paper over-identifies kindhood with projectibility; whether descriptive and classificatory aims get enough space; whether the cross-domain cases support a general philosophy-of-science contribution.
