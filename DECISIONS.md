# DECISIONS

2026-05-28 -- Project scaffolded as a standalone short paper. Reason: Weinberger 2026 supplies a compact theoretical correction to loose HPC/homeostasis talk, and the Boyd/Khalidi relation is recurring across the portfolio.

2026-05-28 -- Use CC BY 4.0 as the repository license. Reason: the primary product is an academic manuscript and notes, not software. CC BY matches open scholarly reuse better than MIT or GPL. Any future substantial code should get its own code-specific license note.

2026-05-28 -- Do not vendor the Springer PDF in the public repo. Reason: even though the article is open access, source PDFs belong in the central `literature/` store, not in every public paper repository. The repo should cite DOI/provenance and point to source anchors.

2026-05-28 -- Initial title: "Not every stable cluster is homeostatic: Control, projectibility, and the Boyd--Khalidi divide." Reason: it states the paper's contribution directly and keeps the article from sounding like a general survey of homeostasis.

2026-05-28 -- Revised subtitle to "Stability, network order, and control in projectible kinds." Reason: feedback from GPT-5.5 Pro and Opus 4.8 made clear that the contribution is the diagnostic distinction among ways projectibility is secured, not primarily the Boyd--Khalidi contrast.

2026-05-28 -- Use the Watt governor as the clean positive control case and glycemia as the harder biological extension. Reason: Weinberger's governor case gives the clearest control-theoretic apparatus, while glycemia raises useful set-point/equilibrium complications.

2026-05-28 -- Keep interjections as the negative linguistic case. Reason: they show stable pragmatic projectibility and some network order, but current evidence does not yet show coordinated corrective control.

2026-05-28 -- Treat the Watt governor as a calibration case, not as a kind. Reason: the paper is about projectible kinds, but the governor supplies the clearest control structure that candidate kinds can be tested against.

2026-05-28 -- Treat network order broadly across domains. Reason: biological and mechanical cases may involve causal order, while linguistic and social cases may involve functional, conventional, sequential, or evidential dependencies.

2026-05-28 -- Add grammaticality as the likely positive linguistic case for future expansion. Reason: interjections are a useful negative case; grammaticality better tests whether correction, repair, acquisition, and transmission can form a coordinated control architecture.
